"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel tells the story of a young girl named Scout and her family in a small Alabama town during the 1930s. Through Scout's eyes, we witness the racial tensions and injustices of the time, as her father, a lawyer, defends a black man accused of rape.

"1984" by George Orwell

This dystopian novel imagines a future in which the government has complete control over people's lives and thoughts. The story follows a man named Winston Smith, who begins to rebel against the oppressive regime and falls in love with a fellow rebel named Julia.

"The Lord of the Rings" by J.R.R. Tolkien

This epic fantasy novel follows a hobbit named Frodo Baggins as he sets out on a quest to destroy the One Ring, which was created by the dark lord Sauron and gives him the power to control all of Middle-earth. Along the way, Frodo is aided by a diverse group of companions, including elves, dwarves, and humans.
